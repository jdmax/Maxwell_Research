Research Summary

One generational gap remains between the confirmed John Maxwells (sons of Alexander/Edward of the Auchincairn line) and John Maxwell of Barfill who fathered Patrick in 1690.

Key Clarifications

The Auchincairn branch — DNA evidence. Don Maxwell's updated DNA chart (May 2026) places kit 752085 at the far right, isolated from all other Maxwell kit clusters, under the label "XV. — Brother of Sir John Maxwell — poss XV. — James Maxwell of Auchincairn." Every other kit in Group 6.48 groups under Archibald of Cowhill and his descendants. Kit 752085 diverges at the level of Sir John's brother, one generation earlier than Archibald. This is the anchor for the revised hypothesis.

Alexander Maxwell & Edward Maxwell — confirmed Auchincairn-era descendants. Two testaments in Dumfries Commissary Court confirm men of the right generation in the right geography. Alexander Maxwell in Towbill-hill, Kirkmahoe (d. November 1625, CC5/6/1) left sons John and Thomas. Edward Maxwell of Isle, Caerlaverock (d. July 1637, CC5/6/2) left son John as sole heir. Both are consistent with being sons of Edward Maxwell of Auchincairn, who is named (as deceased) in Archibald of Cowhill's 1606 will. Kirkmahoe parish is adjacent to both Dumfries and Holywood — the ideal geography for a line ending at Barfill. Neither testament names the father, so the Auchincairn identification rests on Don's DNA research combined with circumstantial evidence.

"Barfie" is a misreading of "Barſill." The true farm name is Barshill or Barfill. In 17th-century Secretary hand the long ſ was commonly misread as f, and terminal il as e. Three independent sources confirm the name: Barfill (RPCS 1684), Barshill (Hearth Tax 1691, Adamson transcript — correct), Barfie (OPR 1690 — misread by clerk). The farm sits in Holywood parish between Hulton and Portrack in the 1684 parish list.

The Stirling Associate congregation error on FamilySearch. FamilySearch indexes Adam Maxwell's 1749 baptism as "Erskine, Renfrewshire" — wrong. The congregation was the Stirling Associate church in Stirling (founded 1733 by Rev. Ebenezer Erskine). ScotlandsPeople correctly identifies it as CH3/559/17, Stirling.

WILL-0 ⭐

Testament of Archibald Maxwell of Cowhill, 1607 ✓ ⭐

Edinburgh Commissary Court · CC8/8/43, pages 692–697 · Registered 1607

Archibald Maxwell of Cowhill died September 1606. Names children: William (eldest heir), Archibald (executor, later c1571–1647), Robert (youngest), John (£500 legacy), Edward (500 merks), Jealls and Elizabeth (daughters). Also names wardship of William and Alexander Maxwell, sons of the late Edward Maxwell of Auchincairne — Archibald undertook their guardianship, with Robert (youngest son) as secondary guardian per the will.

The wardship clause is the documentary foundation for the Auchincairn hypothesis: Edward Maxwell of Auchincairn was deceased by 1606, leaving minor sons. Don's chart identifies these sons (Alexander and Edward per his email) as the line leading to kit 752085.

WILL-1a ⭐

Testament of Alexander Maxwell, Towbill-hill, Kirkmahoe — 1626 ✓ ⭐

Dumfries Commissary Court · CC5/6/1 · Images 183–184 · Confirmed 22 May 1626

"The Testament testamentar … of umquhile Alexander Maxwell in Towbill-hill within the parochin of Kirkmahoe" · Died November 1625. Left goods to his wife and sons John and Thomas Maxwell. Debts include "to his sone John Maxwell iij li."

Significance: Alexander Maxwell died in Kirkmahoe parish — immediately adjacent to both Dumfries town and Holywood parish — roughly 19 years after Archibald's 1606 will. His son John Maxwell born ~1610–1620 would be the right age to be the father of John of Barfill (~b.1654). The modest farming estate is consistent with the social standing of Archibald's ward.

Note on indexing: ScotlandsPeople indexes this as "in Corbellie" — a misread of "Towbill-hill" in the original hand. The document is authoritative.

WILL-1b ⭐

Testament of Edward Maxwell of Isle, Caerlaverock — 1638 ✓ ⭐

Dumfries Commissary Court · CC5/6/2 · Images 23–25 · Will made 9 May 1630 · Died July 1637 · Confirmed 23 May 1638

"I Edward Maxwell of Isle … I nominate John Maxwell my sone my onlie air and executor to my haill guidis and geir." Held Isle farm in Caerlaverock parish as tenant of "my lady of Nithsdale" for "the landis of Kirkblane." Estate: ~£780 (substantial for a farm tenant).

Witnesses to the 1630 will: Edward Maxwell of Lamingtoun (a known figure linked to the Newlaw/Balmangan/Breoch Maxwell networks) and William Maxwell.

Significance: Edward Maxwell of Isle, dying 1637 in Caerlaverock parish (south of Dumfries), is consistent with being one of the Auchincairn minor sons from the 1606 will. His son John Maxwell (already named as heir in 1630 when the will was made, so born by ~1615) is a second candidate to be the father of John of Barfill. The Nithsdale tenancy connection is notable given that Nithsdale Maxwell figures appear in contemporaneous Dumfries OPR records.

Dead Ends & Superseded Hypotheses

The following hypotheses were pursued and then superseded. They are retained here as a research record and because the source documents remain relevant to the broader Maxwell landscape.

The Freircarse Chain Hypothesis — superseded by Don Maxwell's DNA chart (May 2026)
Earlier research proposed the line ran: Archibald of Cowhill (d.1606) → John Maxwell (son, £500 legacy) → John Maxwell of Freircarse (d.1630, Dunscore) → Herbert Maxwell of Freircarse → Jon Maxwell cordiner (Dumfries) → John Maxwell (b.1654) → John of Barfill. Don's updated chart shows kit 752085 branching at the level of Sir John's brother, not Sir John's grandson — one generation earlier than this chain assumed. Additionally, every link in the Freircarse chain was unconfirmed by documents: no source names any father-child relationship across any of the proposed steps. The chain is abandoned.
The Carnsalloch Testament Misidentification
The testament in CC5/6/1 confirmed 31 August 1630 (images 476–478) was previously cited as proof of "John Maxwell of Freircarse." On careful reading it is actually for "Johnne Maxwell in Carnsalloch within the parochin of Kirkmahoe" — a completely different man in a different parish. The genuine testament of John Maxwell of Freircarse (indexed separately in CC5/6/1 as "of Freircars, parish of Dunscoir") exists but was not re-read following the DNA chart reorientation.
Jon Maxwell Cordiner as Direct Link to John of Barfill
Jon Maxwell cordiner, active in Dumfries OPR 821 (children 1654–1676), was proposed as the father of John Maxwell of Barfill. The John Maxwell baptized 13 August 1654 (OPR-B5) was proposed as = John of Barfill. These links were always conjectural and are not pursued in the current hypothesis. The cordiner family is documented in Dumfries records but not connected to the Auchincairn branch by any document.
