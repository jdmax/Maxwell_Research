## The Proposed Scottish Chain (Documentary Evidence, Not All Confirmed)

Working backward from Patrick Maxwell (1690, Holywood):

### Generation: John Maxwell of Barfie (~1654–post 1694)
- Held farm of Barfie, Holywood parish, Dumfriesshire
- Wife: Margaret Thomson (daughter of John Thomson)
- **Proposed identity:** John Maxwell baptized **13 August 1654, Dumfries** (OPR 821/10-152), son of **Jon Maxwell, cordiner** (shoemaker)
- Age check: born August 1654 → age 36 when Patrick born September 1690 ✓
- This is the **ONE REMAINING GAP** — no marriage, death, or testament record found bridging John Maxwell (b.1654, Dumfries) to John Maxwell of Barfie (Holywood, 1690)

### Generation: Jon Maxwell, cordiner, Dumfries (~1620–1640 born)
- Baptized son John on 13 August 1654 in Dumfries (OPR 821/10-152)
- Also had children in Dumfries 1667–1676: Johne (1667, died young), Johne (1669, Woodhead, possibly illegitimate), **Johne (4 Aug 1670, with Robert Maxwell of Carnsalloch as witness)**, Marione (1671), Robert (1673), Johne (1673, died young), Margrat (1674), Agnes (1675), Marione (1676)
- **Brother: William Maxwell** (same father Herbert of Freircarse) — had Jonet (31 Mar 1649), William (2 Apr 1657), Issabell (19 Jul 1670) all in Dumfries OPR 821. Issabell's baptism is on the **same page** (OPR 821/10-184) as Jon Maxwell's August 1670 son — confirming brothers baptizing children together in Dumfries summer 1670

### Generation: Herbert Maxwell of Freircarse (~1610–pre-1647)
- Source: **Index to Particular Register of Sasines, Dumfries (1617–1671), page 259**
- Entry: *"Maxwell, [Elizabeth] Stirling, spouse of William, son of Herbert M. of Freircarse — 3 Aug 1647, Vol.5, Fol.194"*
- Herbert had son **William Maxwell** who married **Elizabeth Stirling** in August 1647
- The Stirling family connection threads through: "Sand Stirling" was cautioner in John Maxwell of Freircarse's 1630 testament; 17 years later William Maxwell married Elizabeth Stirling
- Herbert almost certainly one of the **minor children** (see below) of John Maxwell of Freircarse

### Generation: John Maxwell of Freircarse (d. 1630)
- Source: **Dumfries Commissary Court CC5/6/1** (testament confirmed 31 August 1630)
- Held Freircarse farm, Dunscore parish, Dumfriesshire
- Died 1630 leaving **minor children** ("minorities" visible in confirmation section)
- Sister: "Jonat Innergand, my Sister" — possibly connected to James Maxwell de Innerweek (1643 retour)
- Cautioner: "Sand Stirling" (Stirling family, later connected through the 1647 marriage above)
- **Sasines index page 192:** John Kirkpatrick held Freircarse 1618–1626 (immediately before John Maxwell); transition likely via the Kirkpatrick family connection — Archibald Maxwell of Cowhill's wife was **Margaret Kirkpatrick**
- **Proposed identity:** Son of "John Maxwell, my son" named in Archibald Maxwell of Cowhill's 1606 will

### Generation: John Maxwell, son of Archibald of Cowhill (d.1606)
- Source: **Edinburgh Commissary Court CC8/8/43** (Archibald Maxwell's 1607 testament)
- Archibald's will names: "JTem J leiff to **johone maxwell my sone** v c li~" — £500 to son John
- All children named: William (eldest, heir), Archibald (executor = later Archibald c1571–1647), Robert (youngest), **John (£500)**, Edward (500 merks), Jealls (daughter), Elizabeth (daughter)
- John Maxwell (son) born ~1560–1580; right age to hold Freircarse by 1618 and die there in 1630

### Generation: Archibald Maxwell of Cowhill & Dinwoodie (~1530–1606)
- Documented in Book of Caerlaverock p.594 and his 1607 testament
- Father: Sir John Maxwell of Cowhill (~1500–1547)
- **IMPORTANT:** Archibald's other son Archibald (c1571–1647) → John Maxwell of Cowhill (m. Anna Elliot) → **three daughters, no sons** (Book of Caerlaverock confirmed). Our Y2357+ line runs through John (£500 legacy), not through the main Cowhill line.

## Key Contextual Facts

**Why Robert Maxwell of Carnsalloch witnessed the 1670 Dumfries baptism:**
Book of Caerlaverock p.602 states Agnes Irving (first wife of John Maxwell of Barncleuch) married Robert Maxwell of Carnsalloch as her second husband. This placed Robert squarely in the social network of the Barncleuch/Dumfries Maxwell community, explaining his witness role — NOT a patrilineal connection (Carnsalloch is Y2357 negative, a different branch).

**Robert Maxwell of Porttrack (1696, Holywood):**
Descended from Homer Maxwell (brother of Robert Maxwell of Cowhill) → Maxwells of Portrack. Homer Maxwell's line received Holywood Abbey lands in 1569. Robert Maxwell of Porttrack held Porttrack House in Holywood parish alongside John Maxwell of Barfie — both in the same community but different sub-branches.

**John Maxwell of Cowhill had no sons:**
Book of Caerlaverock p.594 explicitly: "He had three daughters, but no sons." His daughter Janet married Dugald Maxwell (youngest son of Sir Patrick Maxwell of Newark), and their daughter Janet married Charles Murray of Barncleburie who became "Charles Maxwell alias Murray of Cowhill." Confirmed by Dumfries Commissary Court testament CC5/6/9 (Charles Maxwell d.1725).

**The Barncleuch Maxwells:**
Separate from our line. John Maxwell of Barncleugh (d.1722) had son James Maxwell of Barncleugh (executor) — confirmed in Dumfries Commissary Court CC5/6/8. The Barncleuch line descends from Thomas Maxwell, younger son of John Maxwell tutor of Kirkconnell (Book of Caerlaverock p.602).

**Adam's siblings in Stirling:**
All baptized in Stirling Associate congregation (CH3/559/17): John (b.1 Mar 1748), **Adam (b.20 Aug 1749)**, Margret (b.5 Oct 1751), Bolkrak/Barbara (b.15 Dec 1753), Daniel (b.24 Nov 1756), Katharine (b.27 Mar 1760). Family stayed in Stirling at least 1748–1760; Adam emigrated as a young adult.

## The Remaining Gap — Lines of Inquiry

The gap is: **John Maxwell (b.13 Aug 1654, Dumfries, son of Jon Maxwell cordiner)** → **John Maxwell of Barfie (Holywood, father of Patrick b.1690)**

### Inquiry 1: Dumfries Burgh Cordiners' Incorporation Records
Jon Maxwell cordiner would appear in Dumfries trade guild records. These are held at **Dumfries Archive Centre** (not on ScotlandsPeople). His guild entry would name his family, training, and possibly his father. Contact: Dumfries Archive Centre, 33 Burns Street, Dumfries, DG1 2PS. Reference: Records of the Incorporated Trades of Dumfries.

### Inquiry 2: Holywood Kirk Session Records
The Holywood Kirk Session minutes (not fully indexed online) are held at the National Records of Scotland (Edinburgh). John Maxwell of Barfie would appear as a communicant, possible disciplinary case, or in session business. These records sometimes give ages or origins. NRS reference: CH2/184.

### Inquiry 3: Vol. 5, Fol. 194 of the Dumfriesshire Sasines
The actual sasine entry for "William, son of Herbert Maxwell of Freircarse, married Elizabeth Stirling, 3 August 1647" — the full text (not just the index entry) would name the lands and all parties, possibly naming Herbert's other children including Jon Maxwell. NRS holds the original volumes.

### Inquiry 4: Dumfries Commissary Court — Wider Search
Search for Jon Maxwell cordiner's testament, which would name his wife and children. Try spelling variants: "Jon," "Jone," "Johne." Try years 1680–1720. Also search Edinburgh Commissary Court (CC8) in case it was registered there. Online at ScotlandsPeople.

### Inquiry 5: Scottish Valuation Rolls / Hearth Tax
The Scottish Hearth Tax of 1691 (published and searchable) lists householders in each parish. Search for Maxwell entries in Holywood parish — John Maxwell of Barfie should appear. This would place him in Holywood in 1691 and might give his status (exempt = poor; paying = householder). Available at NRS and some published transcriptions.

### Inquiry 6: Dunscore OPR — Any Surviving Records
Dunscore (parish 210) OPR records are sparse but check whether any survive for 1660–1690, which might include the Freircarse family. On ScotlandsPeople, extend the search range.

### Inquiry 7: Marriage Contract Register
In Scotland, marriage contracts were sometimes registered in the Sheriff Court Books of Session. A marriage contract between John Maxwell and Margaret Thomson registered in Dumfries Sheriff Court Books would be definitive. Search ScotlandsPeople Legal Records → Register of Deeds (Dumfries Sheriff Court), 1685–1695.

### Inquiry 9: Maxwell of Freircarse Documentary Trail
Search for any document naming a son of Herbert Maxwell of Freircarse besides William. Specifically:
- Search Dumfries OPR births 1640–1660 for "Herbert Maxwell" as father (would show Herbert's children including Jon)
- Search Dumfries marriages 1660–1680 for Jon Maxwell
- Search Dunscore/Dumfries Kirk Session records for any Maxwell of Freircarse family after 1647

### Inquiry 10: Adam Maxwell's Revolutionary War Pension File
Available on Fold3.com — search "Adam Maxwell" Pennsylvania Revolutionary War. The pension file (S-series or W-series) contains biographical testimony Adam gave in his 70s about his life. It may mention Scottish parentage, when the family came to America, or other details. His pension was filed approximately 1818–1832. Some files name parents explicitly.
