Key Clarifications from New Research

⚠ The Freircarse testament (WILL-1) was misidentified. The testament in CC5/6/1 confirmed 31 August 1630, previously cited as proof of "John Maxwell of Freircarse," is actually for "Johnne Maxwell in Carnsalloch within the parochin of Kirkmahoe" — a completely different man in a different parish. The Carnsalloch testament names his executor as his eldest son John Maxwell, and his tutors as "my mother and my brother." Edward Maxwell appears as both witness and debtor. The genuine testament of John Maxwell of Freircarse, if one was confirmed, has not yet been located. This error removes the documentary proof for generation 17 in the chain.

⚠ The chain from Archibald of Cowhill to Jon Maxwell cordiner is conjectural at every link. Three successive unconfirmed connections were previously presented as confirmed: (A) Archibald's son John → John of Freircarse; (B) John of Freircarse → Herbert; (C) Herbert → Jon Maxwell cordiner. No single document names a parent-child relationship across any of these links. The 2020 Cowhill family chart shows "John Maxwell of Broomholm" as a son of Archibald with line "not traced" — possibly the £500 John, but not connected to Freircarse in any source. The Y-DNA evidence (Y2357 positive, GD2 from Caerlaverock) independently confirms descent from the Cowhill/Portrack branch, but the specific documentary path is unproven.

"Barfie" is a palaeographic misreading of "Barſill." The true farm name is Barshill or Barfill. In 17th-century Secretary hand, the long ſ was commonly misread as f, and terminal il as e, producing "Barfie" from "Barſil." All three historical spellings now resolve consistently: Barfill (RPCS 1684), Barshill (Hearth Tax 1691 Adamson transcript — correct reading), Barfie (OPR 1690 — misread by clerk). A company registered as "Barshill Farm Ltd" confirms the name survives in its correct form.

RPCS Vol. IX, p.300 (1684): Barfill confirmed in Holywood, residents absent. The Register of the Privy Council of Scotland, Third Series, Vol. IX (June–September 1684), page 300, contains a complete list of parishioners for Holywood taken 13 August 1684. The farm of Barfill appears as "Ab. Barfill" — residents absent, unnamed. Every other occupied Maxwell holding in the parish is listed with full names: Robert Maxwell of Killilung (wife Janett Milligan), Edward Maxwell of Muligounton, Robert Maxwell of Steelston, Robert Maxwell of Bearcroft, and others. Barfill is the only holding where the occupant went unnamed, confirming the farm existed in 1684 but not identifying its tenant.

John Maxwell of Cowhill had only daughters. The Book of Caerlaverock (p.594) explicitly states that John Maxwell of Cowhill (m. Anna Elliot) "had three daughters, but no sons." Our Y2357+ male line therefore does not pass through him.

The Stirling Associate congregation error on FamilySearch. FamilySearch indexes Adam Maxwell's 1749 baptism as "Erskine, Renfrewshire" — this is wrong. The congregation was the Stirling Associate church in Stirling, Stirlingshire (founded 1733 by Rev. Ebenezer Erskine). ScotlandsPeople correctly identifies it as CH3/559/17, Stirling.

The Carnsalloch witness explained. Robert Maxwell of Carnsalloch (witness in the 1670 Dumfries baptism, OPR-B4) was not in our patrilineal line. The Book of Caerlaverock (p.602) explains his connection: Agnes Irving (widow of John Maxwell of Barncleuch) married Robert Maxwell of Carnsalloch as her second husband — placing him within the same Dumfries Maxwell social network.

Research Gaps — Multiple Unconfirmed Links
GAP A — Archibald's son John → John Maxwell of Freircarse
No document names John of Freircarse's father. The connection to the Cowhill family is inferred from geographic proximity, timing, and the Kirkpatrick family link (Archibald's wife was Margaret Kirkpatrick; a Kirkpatrick held Freircarse 1618–1626). They may be the same person (Archibald's son John = John of Freircarse) or father and son. The 2020 Cowhill chart shows "John Maxwell of Broomholm" as a son of Archibald with line "not traced" — possibly the same man under a different designation.
GAP B — John Maxwell of Freircarse → Herbert Maxwell of Freircarse
Herbert is confirmed in the 1647 Sasines. John of Freircarse (d.1630) is probable from the Sasines index (Kirkpatrick held Freircarse until 1626; a Maxwell held it by 1630). The father-son connection is inferred from the same farm name across two generations. The testament in CC5/6/1 (31 Aug 1630) previously cited as proof of John of Freircarse is actually for John Maxwell in Carnsalloch, Kirkmahoe — a misidentification. No genuine Freircarse testament has been located.
GAP C — Herbert Maxwell of Freircarse → Jon Maxwell cordiner
Herbert is confirmed as father of William Maxwell (Sasines 1647). Jon Maxwell cordiner is confirmed in OPR 821. The identification of the OPR William Maxwell as Herbert's son William is probable but unproven. The claim that Jon Maxwell cordiner is William's brother rests solely on their baptism entries appearing on the same OPR page in 1670. No document names Jon Maxwell's father.
GAP D — John Maxwell (b.1654, Dumfries) → John Maxwell of Barfill (Holywood, 1690)
This was originally identified as the single gap. A man born 13 August 1654 would be 36 when Patrick was born in 1690 — the right age. The farm of Barfill is confirmed in Holywood parish from 1684 (RPCS Vol IX, p.300: "Ab. Barfill"). No marriage or death record for John Maxwell of Barfill survives in indexed OPR records 1683–1698. No testament found in Dumfries Commissary Court. Almost certainly lost due to incomplete OPR coverage and the low probability of testament confirmation for farm tenants.
